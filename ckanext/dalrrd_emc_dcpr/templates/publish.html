Electronic Metadata Catalogue (EMC)
The Spatial Data Infrastructure Act (Act 54 of 2003) establishes the electronic metadata catalogue (EMC) as a key component of the South African Spatial Data Infrastructure (SASDI). In terms of section 12 of the Act, a data custodian must:
  • Capture and maintain metadata for their spatial information holdings;
  • Ensure that metadata is available to users by:
    • Making it available to the Department, in a prescribed manner, for inclusion in the electronic metadata catalogue; and
    • Including it in a manual on functions as prescribed in section 14 of the Promotion of Access to Information Act, (Act 2 of 2000).
The purpose of the EMC is to provide a mechanism for data custodians to make spatial data available to users as required in terms of section 12 (2) of the Act

Metadata publication

A registered and authenticated user can publish metadata using the following mechanisms:

Manual publication
A registered and authenticated user can publish metadata of their data holdings manually by completing a SANS 1878-1:Metadata compliant form.
XML upload
A registered and authenticated user can publish metadata of their data holdings manually by uploading metadata contained in a SANS 1878-1:Metadata compliant XML file.
Harvesting
A registered and authenticated user can publish metadata by setting up a CSW-compliant harvesting source that provides SANS 1878-1:Metadata compliant records.
Not Authorized

Only authorised users are able to publish metadata records, you don't have the required permissions to complete this action
